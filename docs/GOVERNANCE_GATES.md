# GOVERNANCE GATES

This document summarizes how governance and empirical gates constrain action in the $T$T system.

---

## Purpose

No strategy, council output, or AI recommendation should float free of governance and empirical constraints.

This file exists to summarize those boundaries.

---

## Constitutional Order

1. M1 — constitutional authority
2. M8 — governance state authority
3. M9 — empirical truth authority
4. M10 — memory/orchestration
5. downstream advisory layers

---

## Core Governance Gate States

Examples of practical system states:
- `NORMAL`
- `CAUTION`
- `RESTRICTED`
- `PAUSED`

These should constrain downstream options.

---

## Fail-Closed Rule

If governance or policy implies fail-closed behavior:
- AI may explain
- AI may summarize
- AI may recommend caution
- AI may not manufacture approval

---

## Empirical Gate Rule

If M9 evidence does not support legitimacy:
- council cannot convert weakness into proof
- strategy cannot be treated as deployment-ready
- “less bad” is not the same thing as “approved”

---

## Research vs Deployment Rule

A strategy may be:
- interesting
- narrowing
- less bad
- worth further validation

without being:
- deployable
- governance-approved
- empirically sufficient
